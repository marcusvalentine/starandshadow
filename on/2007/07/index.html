Film: Climate Action Camp - Voices From The Camp For Climate Action-2006

3 Jul 2007, 6 p.m.

In a world driven by profit, humanity seems unwilling to change the course of the fossil fuel economy. In the summer 2006, the hottest year ever recorded in recent history, 600 people convinced that there was no time to waste set up a camp for climate action outside of the Drax coal-fired power station in Yorkshire. It became a space of collective learning, sustainable living and taking direct action on the root causes of climate change. After the film we can discuss the action planned for summer 2007. We have less than 10 years to radically reduce our carbon emissions to avoid catastrophic climate change. The time to act is now. This summer, be part of it. Get involved!

Gig: Ted Leo And The Pharmacists (Usa), Lake Me (Newcastle), Young Soul Rebels (Brighton)

4 Jul 2007, 8 p.m.

Ted Leo and the Pharmacists formed in Washington D.C in 1999. Ted Leo was formerly in mod/pop/dc punk band Chisel. The Pharmacists have previously included members of The Evens, Trans Am and Tsunami, and for this tour welcome back second guitar player James Canty (formerly of The Make Up and Nation of Ulysses, currently of Dischord Records faves French Toast). The new album "living with the living" has just been released on Touch and Go Records. Their music is essentially punk/indie, but incorporates elements of mod, folk, dub and experimental music. This night at The Star and Shadow has been arranged at the special request of the band to make up for their cancellation at the Cluny 2 years ago. Doors at 8pm. Tickets are £5, available from Alt Vinyl, Beatdown Records and RPM.

12

Film: Guca For International Brass Festival (2005)

12 Jul 2007, 7:30 p.m.

Guca (pronounced Gucha) is the name of a small village in Serbia, which for over 40 years has been home to the national trumpet festival. Once a small local affair, it now pulls crowds of over 200,000. The Director Milivoj Ilic's account of the place, the people and the competitors in 2005 is as exuberant, joyous and noisy as the festival itself. Milvoivoj Ilic will be at the screening to talk about the film afterwards. Showing as part of BRASS - Durham International Festival.

14

Festival: Cube-Cola Lab And Soiree

14 Jul 2007, noon

The Cube Microplex (Bristol) is possibly the only volunteer-run cinema in the UK to manufacture its own cola inhouse. The Cube's Kayle Brandon and Kate Rich present a realtime, DIY workshop on how to make Cube-Cola from raw materials, using an open source recipe and techniques siphoned from the worlds of laboratory science and cake-making. No food technology expertise required. Test tubes, hammer drill and kitchen whisks will be provided. Cube-Cola Soiree. 6pm. The fruits of the day's labour will be sampled in an après-lab openhouse, the Star and Shadow bar, where we will blind-taste Cube-Cola with a number of major market competitors. All welcome, bring your own cocktail umbrellas! Further information on Cube-Cola: http://sparror.cubecinema.com/cube/cola To book a place for the lab please email: sneha@ptechnic.org Sneha (Polytechnic) sneha@ptechnic.org
